Datasets

Incident Records

The dataset contains the details of every incident responded to by the London Fire Brigade since January 2009. Information is provided for when and where the incident happened and the type of incident dealt with.


Collected from the London Datastore

Source

Mobilisation Records

The dataset contains the details of every fire engine (pumping appliance) sent to an incident by the London Fire Brigade since January 2009. Information is provided for the appliance mobilised, where it was deployed from and the times recorded for arriving at the incident.

Collected from the London Datastore

Source

Bariatric Incidents

The dataset contains information about all non-fire (special services) incidents attended by the London Fire Brigade since January 2009. These special services include assistance to other emergency services, sometimes help with moving larger (i.e. bariatric) patients/people.

Collected from the London Datastore

Source

Postcodes in the UK

The dataset contains geographical information about postcodes in the UK. The dataset is used to enrich the incident dataset.

Originated from The Office for National Statistics (ONS)

Source

Response times

The average response times are different for each borough. They also change over the years! There seems to be a pattern where centrally located boroughs usually have a lower average response time, while the outer boroughs have the highest response times.

Bob
Firefighter in London
“It's interesting to see the differences in response times between the boroughs! Did you notice that Kensington and Chelsea always have the lowest respone time while Hillingdon always has the highest?”

Try pushing the play button!

Lets get Oliver to investigate! The average response time in Chelsea and Kensington is 275 seconds but in Hillingdon it is 390 seconds, almost a two minute difference. Oliver created a plot of each wards' average response time for those two boroughs. Then he added the fire station locations as black stars and plotted each incident in yellow.

Oliver
Response Time Investigator
“The size scale clearly makes a difference. The distance from the incident location to the station is much greater in Hillingdon. Note that wards that have a fire station usually have a shorter response time than other wards. Duh!”
Chris
Bob's competitive friend
